# Research Paper Template - APA Format

## General Formatting Guidelines

- Use 12-point Times New Roman font
- Double-space the entire document
- Include 1-inch margins on all sides
- Include a running head at the top of every page
- Page numbers in the upper right corner

## Title Page

```
[Title of Research Paper]

[Your Name]
[Institutional Affiliation]
[Course Number and Name]
[Instructor Name]
[Assignment Due Date]
```

## Abstract

Begin a new page. The abstract page should contain:
- A running head
- The page number
- The title of the paper
- The word "Abstract" (centered)
- A brief, comprehensive summary (150-250 words)

## Main Body

### Introduction
- Opening with the problem statement
- Present the research questions
- Explain the significance of the study
- Present the thesis statement or hypothesis

### Literature Review
- Summarize relevant literature
- Identify gaps in current knowledge
- Establish the context for your study

### Methodology
- Describe the research design
- Explain the procedures used
- Detail the materials, participants, or data sources

### Results
- Present the findings
- Use tables and figures if necessary

### Discussion
- Interpret the results
- Explain the implications
- Acknowledge limitations
- Suggest future research

### References

Begin on a new page titled "References" (centered at the top).

**Basic Format for Journal Articles:**
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. *Title of Periodical*, volume(issue), pages. https://doi.org/xx.xxx/yyyy

**Basic Format for Books:**
Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of work: Capital letter also for subtitle*. Publisher Name.

**Basic Format for Book Chapters:**
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of chapter. In A. Editor & B. Editor (Eds.), *Title of book* (pp. page range). Publisher Name.

**Basic Format for Web Documents:**
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Date). Title of online content: Subtitle. Website Name. URL

## Tables and Figures

- Number tables and figures consecutively throughout the paper
- Provide a brief, italicized title for each table/figure
- Include the table/figure number above the table/figure
- Provide source information below the table/figure if it's adapted from another source

## In-text Citations

- (Author, Year) for one author
- (Author & Author, Year) for two authors
- (Author et al., Year) for three or more authors with first citation; (Author et al., Year) for subsequent citations
- (Author, Year, p. #) for direct quotes

## Additional Guidelines

- Use active voice rather than passive voice
- Avoid biased language
- Be precise and concise
- Use appropriate level of headings based on hierarchy
